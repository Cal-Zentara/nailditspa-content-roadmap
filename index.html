That, plus the people behind the salon, is wide open and ours to take.

The Hook Engine
5 rotating hooks, so the feed never goes stale

One repeated hook burns out fast. So we rotate five. The first job of every post is the same: earn the watch in the first 3 seconds. People save and share for two reasons only, it is satisfying to watch or useful to send a friend. Every hook below is built to be one of those. 92% of people watch with the sound off, so the hook always lives in the text on screen too (Torro, 2026).

★
1. The Transformation (lead with this)
Bare or grown out nails at the start, the finished set at the end, in one clip. The brain wants to see the payoff, so people stay to the end. Before and after reveals are the single strongest format for nail content (CreatorFlow, 2026). Text on screen: "from this to this in one visit."
Satisfying to watch · proves the work
2
The Satisfying Close Up
Macro, slow, no talking. The brush laying down color, the shaping, the final gloss coat. This is the ASMR lane that keeps people watching and rewatching. Pair it with a soft track. Watch time is what the algorithm rewards most (Hootsuite, 2026).
Satisfying to watch · drives rewatches
3
The Reaction
The moment a client sees her nails for the first time. Real surprise and delight stops the scroll faster than any posed shot, and it gets saves because it feels human (Nail Experts University). Hard for a faceless competitor to fake.
Human · gets saves
4
The Curiosity
Name a problem she did not know she had. "The one habit quietly wrecking your gel manicure." She has to keep watching to find out. This is the useful to send lane, one friend shares it with another. Curiosity gap hooks travel across every niche (CreatorFlow).
Useful to send · gets shares
💬
5. The Question (our signature close)
End by asking for a quick answer. "Drop the color you would get this summer 👇 we will design a set around it." People cannot resist answering, which feeds the algorithm and quietly shows we do any look. Pair this close with any hook above.
Useful · drives comments and DMs
The filter for every post: would a woman save this to show her tech, or send it to a friend? If it is not satisfying enough to rewatch or useful enough to send, it does not go out. Rotate the five hooks down the calendar so no two weeks feel the same.
✿
Monthly Anchor — The People
Once a month, put a face to the salon, the thing the faceless competitors never do. Spotlight one team member: how long Twee, Nancy, or Mindy has been here, what she is known for, and the clients who only book her. Shoot it over real salon footage so it feels like a place, not an ad. This is the trust engine that turns a follower into a regular, and it quietly carries the Vietnamese owned craft without ever sounding like a tagline.
Once a month · the lane nobody else has

Audience Research, June 2026
How to grow the audience fastest

The calendar fills the feed. This is how you get people nearby to actually see it. Ranked fastest first, pulled from 2024 to 2026 marketing data. Almost all of it is free.

1
Pour into Google reviews first
For a local salon, Google is where the booking actually happens. Nail'd It is strong on Yelp (229 reviews) but thin on Google (29), and Google is what AI assistants and Maps pull from most (our findability audit, April 2026). Getting to 50 plus is the single biggest ranking move available. A one star jump can lift bookings by 9 to 12% (Marketing LTB, 2026). Ask every happy client at checkout with the QR code, and reply to all of them.
Free · fastest payoff · review automation already running
2
Repost real clients and tag them
When a client posts her nails, repost it and tag her. It fills the feed with proof, and it puts the salon in front of all of her friends for free. It also makes the next client want to be featured (OpenTable, Hootsuite 2025). The faceless competitors never do this.
Free · compounds every month
3
Tag the location and use local hashtags every time
Add the Stanton location tag and local hashtags like #StantonNails, #OrangeCountyNails, #CerritosNails to every post and reel. Consistent, precise location signals are what put a small salon into nearby Explore feeds (BulkyFans Local Density Playbook, 2025).
Free · local reach
4
Turn comments into DMs (already live)
CreatorFlow is already running on the page and watches every post. End captions with "Comment BOOK and I will send you our booking link 💅" and it auto DMs the Fresha link the second someone comments, even while you are with a client. Followers barely see your posts. A booking link sitting in their DMs, they act on. Keep BOOK as the everyday keyword so it stays consistent.
Free · CreatorFlow is already set up · keyword BOOK
5
Free sets for small local creators and Facebook groups
Invite a couple of small local creators (1k to 10k followers) in for a free set in exchange for a post. Micro creators have deeper local trust than big ones (MangoMint, Zoca 2026). And quietly join the Stanton and Orange County mom and neighborhood Facebook groups where people ask "best nail place near me." Show up helpful, not spammy.
Free or low cost · builds local word of mouth
Where we start: the page is already live at @naildit.spa, the website and Google profile are up, and review automation is running. That is a warm start. The job is to wake the feed up, post consistently, and make the page worth landing on. Begin with the top two: drive Google reviews (tactic 1) and repost real clients (tactic 2).
Our Edge
Vietnamese owned, the right way

This is the lane no local salon is claiming, but it only works if we show it instead of saying it. "Proudly Vietnamese owned" sitting in the bio is a sticker nobody reads. The rule that keeps it from feeling like marketing: 80% nails, 20% culture. The heritage is the seasoning, not the whole dish.

★
Language is the real unlock
A big share of Stanton and Garden Grove speaks Vietnamese at home. Hearing it is instant comfort. Drop a little Vietnamese into captions and replies naturally, and use a booking CTA like "DM HẸN for a same day spot." This signals without screaming, and it is the most bookable angle we have.
Signals trust · drives local bookings
2
Cultural designs with meaning
Tết and Lunar New Year sets, red for luck and gold for prosperity, explained in one simple line. Show the design and what it means, no personal backstory needed. Start taking Lunar New Year bookings early when the season comes.
Educational · gets saves
3
Show you are part of the community
Tag Little Saigon and Asian Garden Mall, post around local Vietnamese events and holidays. It places the salon inside the community instead of just advertising to it.
Local presence · word of mouth
4
Vietnamese client reactions, subtitled
Film a happy Vietnamese client's reaction in her own words, then add subtitles. It doubles as a reaction post and quietly proves the language and comfort angle without a single marketing line.
Human · proof in their words
5
Bust the rushed myth
The tired stereotype is that a Vietnamese salon means rushed. Flip it: "quality over quota, we take our time." A simple to camera line over real prep footage turns a stereotype into a trust win.
Trust · reframes the category
The line we never cross: no personal family story, no "I learned at my mother's table." We show the craft, the language, the culture, and the team. That is the edge, kept clean.
A Small Lever
Hashtags, honestly

Hashtags do far less than they used to. They will not make a post go viral. But for a local salon they still give a small bump in nearby discovery, so they are worth 30 seconds, not 30 minutes.

Use 5 to 8 on Instagram, local first
  1. Local, lead with these: #StantonNails (barely used, easy to rank), #OCNails, #714Nails, #NorthOC
  2. Rotate nearby cities you serve: #AnaheimNails, #GardenGroveNails, #BuenaParkNails
  3. Match the design: #SummerNails, #ChromeNails, #GelXNails, only when the post actually shows it
  4. One branded tag of our own: #BookNaildIt, and ask clients to use it
  5. On Facebook: 1 to 2 tags max, and tag the Stanton location instead. Facebook punishes hashtag stuffing.
The Short Version
How to grow fast, in 6 steps

The whole plan above, boiled down. If you only remember six things, remember these.

Pin this by the front desk
  1. Post short video 5 times a week. Under 20 seconds, hook in the first second. A phone is fine.
  2. Lead with the before and after. The reveal is the format that always works.
  3. Make people save or send it. End with "save this for your next fill" or "send this to your nail friend."
  4. Show the people. Hands, faces, the team, real clients. That is the whole advantage.
  5. Tag the Stanton location and reply to every comment within the hour. The app rewards it.
  6. Ask for the Google review at checkout, every time. One star up is up to 12% more bookings.
